Welcome to my website!

I am a graduate student at the University of Chicago.

I currently work with Alex Ji on using machine learning to fit NLTE stellar atmosphere spectra.

I went to UCSB for my undergradate degree!

I have a Bachelors of Science in Physics from the College of Creative Studies at the University of California, Santa Barbara. At Santa Barbara, I worked in social media for the Physics department. I also ran an astronomy club and helped organize UDIP, an undergrad-focused equity group.

I am broadly interested in galaxy formation!

At UCSB, I worked with Dr. Crystal Martin to write an image binning code for use on spatially resolved images or spectra. I like technical problems and would like to work somewhere between simulation-driven theory and observational analysis.

I am a creative person!

In my free time, I like to write, draw, and create. I am interested in coding video games using engines such as Unity, or in creating interactive fiction. Maybe you'd like to play a little twine story that I wrote? In 2021, my poetry was published in the Spectrum Literary Journal Summer Edition.
